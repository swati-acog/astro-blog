We are obsessed with 'busy'. We wear our long to-do lists like badges of honor. But after years of juggling a dozen projects at once, I've realized that the most productive people I know actually do the *least*.

### The Power of 'No'
Every time you say 'yes' to something trivial, you are saying 'no' to something essential. Minimalist productivity starts with a ruthless audit of your commitments.

### Deep Work vs. Shallow Work
I've started blocking out 4-hour windows for deep, uninterrupted work. No emails, no Slack, no phone. The output I produce in those 4 hours exceeds what I used to do in a whole week of fragmented 'shuffling'.

### My One-Item To-Do List
Every morning, I pick the **one** task that would make the biggest difference if completed. That's it. Everything else is secondary.
